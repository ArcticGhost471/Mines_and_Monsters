Corpses. When first encountered, a corrupted cauldron contains the corpses of 1d4 humanoid. A corrupted cauldron can hold the remains of up to 4 humanoids. These remains have total cover against attacks and other effects outside the corrupted cauldron. If the corrupted cauldron dies, the corpses within it can be pulled free.

 While it has at least one humanoid corpse in its body, the corrupted cauldron can use a bonus action to do one of the following:

  • The corrupted cauldron absorbs one humanoid corpse in its body and instantly regain 5 (1d10) hit points. Nothing of the absorbed body remains. Any equipment of the corpse is expelled from the corrupted cauldron within 5 feet of it.
  • The corrupted cauldron animates one dead humanoid in its body, turning it into a zombie. The zombie appears in an unoccupied space within 5 feet of the corrupted cauldron and acts immediately after it in the initiative order. The zombie acts as an ally of the corrupted cauldron but isn't under its control.

Innate Spellcasting (1/day). The corrupted cauldron can innately cast wall of fire (spell save DC 12), requiring no material components. It's innate spellcasting ability is Wisdom.


Actions

Multiattack. The corrupted cauldron makes three caustic bubble attacks.


Caustic Bubble. Ranged Spell Attack: +4 to hit, range 60 ft., one target. Hit: 9 (2d6 + 2) Acid damage.


Harvest the Dead. The corrupted cauldron sucks up one unsecured dead humanoid within 10 feet of it and stuffs the corpse into itself, along with any equipment the corpse is wearing or carrying. The remains can be used with the Corpses trait.


Bonus Actions

Summon Brew (1/day). The corrupted cauldron magically summons five pink slimes. The summoned slimes appear in unoccupied spaces within 60 feet of the corrupted cauldron and take their turns immediately after it in the initiative order. Each lasts for 1 hour, until it or the corrupted cauldron dies, or until the corrupted cauldron dismisses it as a bonus action.
