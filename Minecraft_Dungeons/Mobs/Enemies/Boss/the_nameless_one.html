Rejuvenation. If it has a phylactery, the Nameless One gains a new body in 1d10 days when theirs is destroyed, regaining all its hit points and becoming active again. The new body appears within 5 feet of the phylactery.


Spellcasting. The Nameless One is a 20th-level spellcaster. Its spellcasting ability is Intelligence (spell save DC 20, +12 to hit with spell attacks). The Nameless One has the following wizard spells prepared:


Cantrips (at will): chill touch, dancing lights, mage hand, prestidigitation, ray of frost

1st level (4 slots): detect magic, magic missile, shield, thunderwave

2nd level (3 slots): detect thoughts, invisibility, Melf's acid arrow, mirror image

3rd level (3 slots): animate dead, counterspell, dispel magic, fireball

4th level (3 slots): blight, dimension door

5th level (3 slots): cloudkill, mislead, scrying

6th level (2 slots): disintegrate, globe of invulnerability

7th level (2 slots): finger of death, plane shift, project image

8th level (1 slots): dominate monster, power word stun

9th level (1 slots): power word kill


Turn Resistance. The Nameless One has advantage on saving throws against any effects that turn undead.


Actions

Paralyzing Touch. Melee Spell Attack: +12 to hit, reach 5 ft., one creature. Hit: 10 (3d6) cold damage. The target must succeed on a DC 18 Constitution saving throw or be paralyzed for one minute. The target can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success


Legendary Actions

The Nameless One can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action option can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. The Nameless One regains spent legendary actions at the start of its turn.


Cantrip. The Nameless One casts a cantrip.

Paralyzing Touch (Costs 2 Actions). The Nameless One uses its Paralyzing Touch.

Frightening Gaze (Costs 2 Actions). The Nameless One fixes its gaze on one creature it can see within 10 feet of it. The target must succeed on a DC 18 Wisdom saving throw against this magic or become frightened for 1 minute. The frightened target can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success. If a target's saving throw is successful or the effect ends for it, the creature is immune to the Nameless One's gaze for the next 24 hours.

Disrupt Life (Costs 3 Actions). Each living creature within 20 feet of the Nameless One must make a DC 18 Constitution saving throw against this magic, taking 21 (6d6) necrotic damage on a failed save, or half as much on a successful one.


The Nameless One's Lair

The Nameless One reigns over the remains of an nameless kingdom, residing within kingdom's Desert Temple. Though the wind-blown sands of the temple have long since wiped the traces of this vast ancient kingdom, the Nameless One and its subjects still haunt these ruins seeking to reclaim what was once theirs.

 The Nameless One when encountered in its lair has a challenge rating of 22 (41,000 XP).


Lair Actions

On initiative count 20 (losing initiative ties), the Nameless One takes a lair action to cause one of the following magical effects; the Nameless One can't use the same effect two rounds in a row:

  • The Nameless One rolls a d8 and regains a spell slot of that level or lower. If it has no spent spell slots of that level or lower, nothing happens.
  • The Nameless One targets one creature it can see within 30 feet of it. A crackling cord of negative energy tethers the Nameless One to the target. Whenever the Nameless One takes damage, the target must make a DC 18 Constitution saving throw. On a failed save, the Nameless One takes half the damage (rounded down), and the target takes the remaining damage. This tether lasts until initiative count 20 on the next round or until the Nameless One or the target is no longer in the Nameless One's lair.
  • The Nameless One calls forth the spirits of creatures that died in its lair. These apparitions materialize and attack one creature that the lich can see within 60 feet of it. The target must succeed on a DC 18 Constitution saving throw, taking 52 (15d6) necrotic damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a success. The apparitions then disappear.

Actions

Bonus Actions

Protective Magic (3/day). The Nameless One casts Counterspell or Shield in response to the spell's trigger, using the same spellcasting ability as Spellcasting.


Legendary Actions

Legendary Action Uses: 3 (4 in Lair). Immediately after another creature's turn, The Nameless One can expend a use to take one of the following actions. The Nameless One regains all expended uses at the start of each of its turns.


Macabre Teleport. The Nameless One teleports up to 60 feet to an unoccupied space it can see, and each creature within 10 feet of the space it left takes 22 (4d10) Necrotic damage.


Disturb Life. Constitution Saving Throw: DC 22, each creature that isn't an Undead in a 20-foot Emanation originating from the lich. Failure: 42 (12d6) Necrotic damage. Success: Half damage. Failure or Success: The Nameless One can't take this action again until the start of its next turn.


Frightening Gaze. The Nameless One casts Fear, using the same spellcasting ability as Spellcasting. The Nameless One can't take this action again until the start of its next turn.


The Nameless One's Lair

The Nameless One reigns over the wind-blown sands of the Desert Temple, commanding their army of undead subjects against the living world.


Regional Effects

The region containing the Nameless One's lair is warped by its presence, creating the following effects


Dust Storms. Sandstorms rage within 6 miles of the lair. The area is Lightly Obscured.

Forever Undead. Undead creatures that have been slain within 6 miles of the lair revive again with all their Hit Points at dawn.


If the Nameless One dies or moves its lair elsewhere, these effects end immediately. These effects resume if the Nameless One gains a new body (see its Spirit Jar trait).
