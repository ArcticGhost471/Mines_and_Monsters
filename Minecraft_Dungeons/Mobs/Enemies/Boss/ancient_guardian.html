Barbed Hide. At the start of each of its turns, the guardian deals 5 (1d10) piercing damage to any creature grappling it.


Legendary Resistance (3/day). If the guardian fails a saving throw, it can choose to succeed instead.


Magic Resistance. The guardian has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects.


Actions

Multiattack. The guardian makes three tail attacks.


Tail. Melee Weapon Attack: +8 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 13 (2d8 + 4) bludgeoning damage.


Guardian Laser. The guardian shoots a magical laser at a creature it can see within 60 feet from itself. The creature must make a DC 18 Dexterity saving throw, taking 21 (4d6) force damage on a failed save, or half as much on a successful one.


Legendary Actions

The guardian can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action option can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. The guardian regains spent legendary actions at the start of its turn.


Move. The guardian moves up to half its speed.

Tail Swipe. The guardian makes one tail attack.

Biomine (Costs 2 Actions). The guardian conjured a biomine at a point it can see within 120 feet, which detonated in a 20-foot-radius Sphere. Each creature in that sphere must make a DC 18 Charisma saving throw, taking 18 (4d8) Poison damage on a failed save, or half as much on a successful one.


The Ancient Guardian's Lair

The ancient guardian's lair is an flooded structure known as the Abyssal Monument. After falling victim to the Orb of Dominance's influence, the ancient guardian now rampages through the momument it was once tasked with protecting.


Lair Actions

On initiative count 20 (losing initiative ties), the guardian takes a lair action to cause one of the following effects; the guardian can't use the same effect two rounds in a row:

  • Bubble Currents. Water currents surge through the monument in a 30-foot cube within 60 feet of the guardian in a random direction, including upward or downward. Each creature within 5 feet of the cube must make a DC 15 Strength saving throw or be dragged up to 30 feet in the direction of the current.
  • Pressure Surge. Water pressure in the lair shifts suddenly in a 20-foot radius sphere centered on a point the guardian can see within 60 feet. Each creature submerged in water in that sphere must make a DC 15 Constitution saving throw, taking 21 (6d6) bludgeoning damage on a failed save, or half as much on a successful one. On a failed save, the creature is also deafened until the end of its next turn.
  • Prismarine Pulse. The monument's prismarine surfaces pulse with arcane energy. Each creature in contact with a wall, floor, or ceiling must succeed on a DC 15 Constitution saving throw or take 10 (3d6) force damage.

Regional Effect

The region containing the Ancient Guardian's lair is warped by its magic, creating one or more of the following effects:

  • All-Seeing Eye. The guardian can see and hear all that happens in a 1 mile radius centered on the lair. The effect is identical to the clairvoyance spell.
  • Lethargic Wave. Creatures within 1 mile of the lair feel a wave of lethargy wash over them. Whenever a creature other than the guardian or one of its allies finishes a Long Rest in that area, it must succeed on a DC 15 Constitution saving throw or gain 1 Exhaustion level.

If the Ancient Guardian dies, these effects fade over the course of 1d10 days.
