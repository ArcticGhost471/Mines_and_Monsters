Barbed Hide. At the start of each of its turns, the endersent deals 5 (1d10) piercing damage to any creature grappling it.


Implosion. When the endersent drops to 0 hit points, it dies as its body implodes.


Legendary Resistance (3/day). If the endersent fails a saving throw, he can choose to succeed instead.


Magic Resistance. The endersent has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects.


Sunlight Sensitivity. While in sunlight, the endersent has disadvantage on attack rolls.


Water Susceptibility. The endersent takes 1 acid damage for every 5 feet it moves in water, for every gallon of water splashed on it, or whenever it starts its turn in the rain.


Actions

Multiattack. The endersent makes two cleaving slam attacks.


Cleaving Slam. Melee Weapon Attack: +10 to hit, reach 15 ft., one target. Hit: 25 (4d8 + 7) sharpness damage plus 13 (3d8) necrotic damage.


Teleport. The endersent magically teleports, along with any equipment it is wearing or carrying, up to 60 feet to an unoccupied space it can see.


Legendary Actions

The endersent can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action option can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. The endersent regains spent legendary actions at the start of its turn.


Attack. The endersent makes one slam attack.

Teleport. The endersent uses teleport.

Aberrant Hold (Costs 2 Actions). The endersent raises an arm and points at a creature it can see within 60 feet of it. The target must succeed on a DC 16 Wisdom saving throw against this magic or become stunned for 1 minute. The stunned target can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success. If a target's saving throw is successful or the effect ends for it, the creature is immune to the endersent's gaze for the next 24 hours.

Thorny Defense (Costs 3 Actions). The endersent creates shimmering, magical thorns around itself. Any creature that makes a successful attack roll against the endersent take 10 (3d6) force damage until the start of the endersent's next turn.


An Endersent's Lair

An endersent's selects deep caverns or empty dungeons to make into their lairs. They don't wander far from their lairs, choosing to hide their presence from other creatures. As being from far beyond, their presence warps the terrain around the lair to resemble their place of origin.


Regional Effect

The region containing endersent's lair is warped by its presence, creating one or more of the following effects:

  • Disorienting Static. Non-ender creatures within 1 mile of the lair have disadvantage on saving throws to maintain concentration, as static rings in their minds, growing louder the closer they reach the lair.
  • End Watchers. Watchlings keep watchful eyes within 1 mile of the lair, scouring the lands and acting as the endersent's eyes.
  • Incorporeal Roots. Intangible roots of astral essence grow within 1 mile of the lair. Any non-ender creatures that pass through the roots take 7 (2d6) necrotic damage.

If the endersent dies, these effects fade over the course of 1d10 days.

Legendary Action Uses: 3 (4 in Lair). Immediately after another creature's turn, The endersent can expend a use to take one of the following actions. The endersent regains all expended uses at the start of each of its turns.

Deathly Teleport. The endersent teleports up to 60 feet to an unoccupied space it can see, and each creature within 10 feet of the space it left takes 11 (2d10) Necrotic damage.

The endersent's Lair

The endersent takes over caverns or empty dungeons, corrupting them with power from far beyond.


Regional Effects

The region containing the endersent's lair is warped by its presence, creating the following effects


Disorienting Static. Non-ender creatures within 1 mile of the lair have Disadvantage on Concentration Saving Throws.

Incorporeal Roots. Intangible roots of astral essence grow within 1 mile of the lair. Any non-ender creatures that pass through the roots take 7 (2d6) necrotic damage.


If the endersent dies or moves its lair elsewhere, these effects end immediately.
