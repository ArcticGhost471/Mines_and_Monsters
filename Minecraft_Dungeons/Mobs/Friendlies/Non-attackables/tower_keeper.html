Immutable Form. The tower keeper is immune to any spell or effect that would alter its form.


Innate Spellcasting. The tower keeper's spellcasting ability is Charisma (spell save DC 17). It can innately cast the following spells, requiring no material components:


At will: minor illusion

1/day each: elemental weapon, magic weapon


Legendary Resistance (3/day). If the tower keeper fails a saving throw, it can choose to succeed instead.


Magic Resistance. The tower keeper has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects.


Spellcasting. The tower keeper is a 16th-level spellcaster. Its spellcasting ability is Intelligence (spell save DC 19, +11 to hit with spell attacks). The tower keeper has the following spells prepared:


Cantrips (at will): dancing light, mage hand, mending, prestidigitation, ray of frost

1st level (4 slots): detect magic, fog cloud, magic missile, shield

2nd level (3 slots): detect thoughts, invisibility, mirror image

3rd level (3 slots): counterspell, dispel magic, fireball summon undead

4th level (3 slots): dimension door, hallucinatory terrain, leomund's secret chest

5th level (2 slots): hold monster, legend lore, scrying

6th level (1 slots): arcane gate, globe of invulnerability

7th level (1 slots): simulacrum

8th level (1 slots): maze


Actions

Multiattack. The tower keeper makes three arcane burst attacks.


Arcane Burst. Melee or Ranged Spell Attack: +11 to hit, reach 5 ft. or range 120 ft., one target. Hit: 21 (3d10 + 5) force damage.


Teleport. The tower keeper magically teleports, along with any equipment it is wearing or carrying, up to 120 feet to an unoccupied space it can see.


Bonus Actions

Summon Keepers (1/day). The tower keeper magically summons 1d4 Tower Wraiths or 1d2 Tower Guards. The summoned creatures appear in unoccupied spaces within 60 feet of the tower keeper, whom they obey. They take their turns immediately after the tower keeper. Each lasts for 1 hour, until it or the tower keeper dies, or until the tower keeper dismisses it as a bonus action.


Legendary Actions

The tower keeper can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action option can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. The tower keeper regains spent legendary actions at the start of its turn.


Arcane Burst. The tower keeper makes one arcane burst attack.

Cast a Spell (Costs 2 Actions). The tower keeper uses Spellcasting.

Break Concentration (Costs 3 Actions). The tower creature targets a creature it can see within 120 feet. The target's concentration on a spell it has cast or an ability it is maintaining ends.


Tower Keeper's Lair

The tower keeper watches over a tower designed to challenge other for their own entertainment. The so called Challenge Spire reaches far above the clouds and consists of a varying number of floors, each connected by portals to harmless demiplanes rather than by stairs. It and its fellow tower keepers ensure the tower remains in proper condition and ready to revieve its next batch of challengers.


Lair Actions

On initiative count 20 (losing initiative ties), the tower keeper takes a lair action to cause one of the following effects; the tower keeper can't use the same effect two rounds in a row:

  • Disrupting Wave. A magical wave spreads from a point the tower keeper can see within 120 feet of it. Each creature within a 20-foot radius centered on that point must make a DC 19 Constitution saving throw or take 10 (3d6) force damage and be paralyzed until the end of its next turn.
  • Empty Room. One creature the tower keeper can see within 120 feet must succeed on a DC 19 Charisma saving throw or be banished into a harmless demiplane and be incapacitated until initiative count 20 on the next round. When the effect ends, the creature reappears in the space it left or in the nearest unoccupied space if that one is occupied.

Lair Traits

The tower keeper's tower may have any or all of the following effects in place:

  • Concealing Terrain. While within 1 mile of the lair, the tower keeper can alter the terrain at will in a 1-mile radius centered on its lair. The effect is identical to the mirage arcane spell.
  • Safe Haven. When a creature enters the first floor of the tower, the creature becomes warded against attacks until they move up the tower. This effect is identical to the sanctuary spell.
  • Tower Avatar. When a creature enters the tower, instead an ethereal avatar of their body is projected into the tower. The creature's body is magically sent to a harmless demiplane while their avatar explores the tower, reappearing when the creature leaves the tower in the space it left, in the nearest unoccupied space if that one is occupied, or in the space closest to its avater if the avatar left through another exit or by other means. While in this demiplane, the body is unconscious and in a state of suspended animation; it doesn't need food or air and doesn't age. Any damage or other effects that apply to an avatar have no effect on the creature's body and vice versa. If a creature's body or avatar form drops to 0 Hit Points, the effect ends for the creature. When the effect ends, the affected creature returns to its physical body, and it awakens.

If the tower keeper dies, these effects fade over the course of 1d10 days.

Spellcasting. The tower keeper casts one of the following spells, requiring no Material components and using Intelligence as the spellcasting ability (spell save DC 19):


At will: Detect Magic, Fireball (5th level version), Mage Hand, Minor Illusion, Prestidigitation

2/day each: Dimension Door, Plane Shift, Summon Undead

1/day each: Arcane Gate, Legend Lore, Scrying, Simulacrum


Bonus Actions

Teleport. The tower keeper teleports up to 30 feet to an unoccupied space it can see.


Rections

Protective Magic. The tower keeper casts Counterspell or Shield in response to the spell's trigger, using the same spellcasting ability as Spellcasting.


Legendary Actions

Legendary Action Uses: 3. Immediately after another creature's turn, the tower keeper can expend a use to take one of the following actions. The tower keeper regains all expended uses at the start of each of its turns.


Arcane Blast. The tower keeper makes an Arcane Burst attack.


Break Concentration. Charisma Saving Throw: DC 17, one creature maintaining Concentration that the tower keeper can see within 120 feet. Failure: The target's concentration on a spell it has cast or an ability it is maintaining ends. Success: The target has Disadvantage on the next saving throw to maintain Concentration. Failure or Success: The tower keeper can't take this action again until the start of its next turn.


Disrupting Wave. Constitution Saving Throw: DC 19, each creature in a 20-foot-radius centered on a point the tower keeper can see within 120 feet. Failure: 18 (4d8) Force damage and the target has the Paralyzed condition until the end of the tower keeper's next turn. Failure or Success: The tower keeper can't take this action again until the start of its next turn.


Tower Keeper's Lair

The tower keeper oversees a magical tower, the challenge spire, full of combat challenges for its entertainment.


Regional Effects

The region containing Tower Keeper's lair is warped by its magic, creating the following effects:


Concealing Terrain. While in its lair, the tower keeper can cast Mirage Arcane, requiring no spell components and using the same spellcasting ability as its Spellcasting action.

Tower Avatar. When a creature enters the tower, instead an ethereal avatar of their body is projected into the tower. The creature's body is magically sent to a harmless demiplane while their avatar explores the tower, reappearing when the creature leaves the tower in the space it left, in the nearest unoccupied space if that one is occupied, or in the space closest to its avater if the avatar left through another exit or by other means. While in this demiplane, the body is in a state of suspended animation; it has the Unconscious condition, doesn't need food or air, and doesn't age. Any damage or other effects that apply to an avatar have no effect on the creature's body and vice versa. If a creature's body or avatar form drops to 0 Hit Points, the effect ends for the creature. When the effect ends, the creature reappears in its body and exits the state of suspended animation.


If the tower keeper dies or moves his lair elsewhere, these effects end immediately.
