Barbed Hide. At the start of each of its turns, the guardian deals 5 (1d10) piercing damage to any creature grappling it.


Immutable Form. The guardian is immune to any spell or effect that would alter its form.


Legendary Resistance (3/day). If the guardian fails a saving throw, it can choose to succeed instead.


Actions

Multiattack. The guardian makes two tail attacks.


Tail. Melee Weapon Attack: +5 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 5 (1d6 + 2) bludgeoning damage.


Guardian Laser. The guardian shoots a magical laser at a creature it can see within 60 feet from itself. The creature must make a DC 15 Dexterity saving throw, taking 14 (4d6) force damage on a failed save, or half as much on a successful one.


Mining Fatigue. Each creature of the guardian's choice that is within 120 feet of the guardian must succeed on a DC 17 Constitution saving throw or gain a level of exhaustion. If a creature's saving throw is successful or the creature has already been afflicted with Mining Fatigue in the last 24 hours, the creature is immune to the guardian's Mining Fatigue for the next 24 hours. The level of exhaustion is reduced if the Elder Guardian is defeated.


Legendary Actions

The guardian can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action option can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. The guardian regains spent legendary actions at the start of its turn.


Move. The guardian moves up to half its speed.

Tail Swipe. The guardian makes one tail attack.

Spectral Gaze (Costs 2 Actions). The guardian's eye flashes with spectral light as it scours the battlefield. Each creature of the guardian's choice that it can see within 60 feet must make a DC 16 Charisma saving throw or be outlined in yellow light for 1 minute. For the duration, affected creatures shed dim light in a 10-foot radius and their outline is visible through solid objects or total cover. Any attack roll against an affected creature has advantage if the attacker can see it, and the affected creature can't benefit from being invisible or from illusions.


An Elder Guardian's Lair

An Elder Guardian's lair is an ancient structure known as an Ocean Monument. Found deep beneath the water and completely flooded, these ruins are said to contain gold, diamonds, and other precious materials. Each is guarded by a trio of Elder Guardians.


Lair Actions

On initiative count 20 (losing initiative ties), the guardian takes a lair action to cause one of the following effects; the guardian can't use the same effect two rounds in a row:

  • Bubble Currents. Water currents surge through the monument in a 30-foot cube within 60 feet of the guardian in a random direction, including upward or downward. Each creature within 5 feet of the cube must make a DC 15 Strength saving throw or be dragged up to 30 feet in the direction of the current.
  • Pressure Surge. Water pressure in the lair shifts suddenly in a 20-foot radius sphere centered on a point the guardian can see within 60 feet. Each creature submerged in water in that sphere must make a DC 15 Constitution saving throw, taking 21 (6d6) bludgeoning damage on a failed save, or half as much on a successful one. On a failed save, the creature is also deafened until the end of its next turn.
  • Prismarine Pulse. The monument's prismarine surfaces pulse with arcane energy. Each creature in contact with a wall, floor, or ceiling must succeed on a DC 15 Constitution saving throw or take 10 (3d6) force damage.
