Command Block Core. The wither's command block core prevents it from being destroyed. The core is inside the wither's body, keeping it hidden and acting as its lair.


Growing Storm (Recharges after a Short or Long Rest). If the wither is reduced to 0 hit points, it doesn't die or fall unconscious. Instead, the damage creates a hole in its shell, opening a path to its command block core. Its core has an AC of 20 and 200 hit points. It is immune to psychic and poison damage, and it is immune to all conditions. If it is forced to make a saving throw, treat its ability scores as 10 (+0). In addition, the wither splits into three, creating two wither storm progenies which appear in unnocupied spaces within 30 feet of itself. The progenies act as allies of the wither and take their turns immediately after the wither in the initiative order. If the wither finishes a short or long rest, the shell heals, the progenies are absorbed into itself, and the path is sealed. The wither and its progenies die when its core is destroyed. Additionally, the wither can now use the options in the "Mythic Actions" section for 1 hour. Award a party an additional 155,000 XP (310,000 XP total) for defeating the wither after its Growing Storm activates.


Immutable Form. The wither is immune to any spell or effect that would alter its form.


Legendary Resistance (5/day in This Stage). If the wither fails a saving throw, it can choose to succeed instead.


Magic Resistance. The wither has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects.


Rampaging Aftershock. If it has a command block core, the wither gains a new body in 1d4 hours when theirs is destroyed, regaining all its hit points and becoming active again in its third stage. The new body appears within on the space of its command block core.


Siege Monster. The wither deals double damage to objects and structures.


Three Heads. The wither has advantage on Wisdom (perception) checks and on saving throws against being blinded, deafened, stunned, and knocked unconscious.


Actions

Multiattack. The wither can use its Frightful Presence or Tractor Gaze. It then makes six attacks: three with its bite, and three with its tentacles, each tentacle attack it can replace with one use of Fling.


Bite. Melee Weapon Attack: +19 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 23 (3d8 + 10) piercing damage. If the target is a Large or smaller creature grappled by the wither, the creature is swallowed, and the grapple ends. While swallowed, the creature is blinded and restrained, it has total cover against attacks and other effects outside the wither, and it takes 56 (16d6) Necrotic damage at the start of each of the wither's turns.

 If the wither takes 50 damage or more on a single turn from a creature inside it, the wither must succeed on a DC 25 Constitution saving throw at the end of that turn or regurgitate all swallowed creatures, which fall prone in a space within 10 feet of the wither. If the wither dies, a swallowed creature is no longer restrained by it and can escape from the corpse using 30 feet of movement, exiting prone.

 If a swallowed creature is regurgitated, it must succeed on a DC 20 Constitution saving throw or be cursed with mad wither. The cursed target can't recall any memories from before it was consumed by the wither, remembering only eternal darkness. Over the next minute, they'll gradually remember vague details about their past but they'll continue to suffer from retrograde amnesia. The curse can only be dispelled by the wish spell or the death of the wither that inflicted it.


Tentacle. Melee Weapon Attack: +19 to hit, reach 30 ft., one target. Hit: 20 (3d6 + 10) bludgeoning damage, and the target is grappled (escape DC 20). Until this grapple ends, the target is restrained. The wither has eight tentacles, each of which can grapple one target. If the wither scores a critical hit against a creature, the target must succeed on a DC 20 Constitution saving throw or become diseased. The disease has no effect for 1 minute and can be removed by any magic that cures disease. After 1 minute, the diseased creature's skin becomes pale, an area of their skin decays, darkening to a purple hue, the creature can't regain hit points, and the disease can be removed only by wish or the death of the wither that inflicted it. After 10 minutes, the diseased creature becomes poisoned. While the creature is affected by this disease, it takes 5 (1d10) necrotic damage every 10 minutes. If the disease reduces the target's hit points to 0, the target dies, and its body rapidly darkens with decay and their eyes and mouth glow with an unnatural violet hue.


Fling. One Large or smaller object held or creature grappled by the wither is thrown up to 60 feet in a random direction and knocked prone. If a thrown target strikes a solid surface, the target takes 3 (1d6) bludgeoning damage for every 10 feet it was thrown. If the target is thrown at another creature, that creature must succeed on a DC 18 Dexterity saving throw or take the same damage and be knocked prone.


Frightful Presence. Each creature of the wither's choice within 120 feet of it and aware of it must succeed on a DC 21 Wisdom saving throw or become frightened for 1 minute. A creature can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, with disadvantage if the wither is within line of sight, ending the effect on itself on a success. If a creature's saving throw is successful or the effect ends for it, the creature is immune to the wither's Frightful Presence for the next 24 hours.


Tractor Gaze. The wither uses its three tractor beams, choosing one to three targets it can see within 90 feet of it. If the target is a creature, it must succeed on a DC 27 Strength saving throw or be pulled up to 30 feet straight towards the wither.


Legendary Actions

The wither can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action option can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. The wither regains spent legendary actions at the start of its turn.


Tentacle. The wither makes one tentacle attack.

Tractor Gaze (Costs 2 Actions). The wither uses its tractor gaze.

Bite (Costs 3 Actions). The wither makes one bite attack.


Mythic Actions

If the wither's Growing Storm trait is active, it can use the options below as legendary actions.


Lash. The wither makes two tentacle attacks.

Wither Storm (Costs 2 Actions). The wither rages a storm. Each creature in a 120-foot-radius sphere centered on the wither must succeed on a DC 27 Constitution saving throw or take 19 (3d12) necrotic damage and be poisoned until the end of its next turn.


An Wither Storm's Lair

The wither storm is its own lair. As it absorbs its surroundings, its body becomes a living dungeon in order to defend its command block core. A purple hue covers the air and surfaces of the lair, as the fleshy mix of organic and inorganic material pulses in beat with its undead core.


Lair Actions

On initiative count 20 (losing initiative ties), the wither takes a lair action to cause one of the following effects; the wither can't use the same effect two rounds in a row:

  • Cloud of Decay. A cloud of stench and decay forms in a 20-foot-radius sphere centered on a point the wither can see within 120 feet of it. The cloud spreads around corners, and its area is lightly obscured. It lasts until initiative count 20 on the next round. Each creature that starts its turn in the cloud must succeed on a DC 15 Constitution saving throw or be poisoned until the end of its turn. While poisoned in this way, a creature also takes 10 (3d6) Necrotic damage at the start of its turn.
  • Command Kill. A magical burst of energy targets a creature the wither can see within 120 feet of it. The creature must succeed on a DC 15 Constitution saving throw or take 21 (6d6) Force damage and be stunned until the end of its next turn.
  • Writhing Flesh. Fleshy tendrils and tentacles erupt in a 20-foot radius centered on a point in the ground the wither can see within 120 feet of it. The area becomes difficult terrain, and each creature there must succeed on a DC 15 Strength saving throw or be restrained by the tendrils. A creature can be freed if it or another creature takes an action to make a DC 15 Strength check and succeeds. The tendrils and tentacles retract when the wither uses this lair action again or when the wither dies.

Regional Effect

The region containing a wither storm's lair is warped by the wither's presence, creating the following effects:

  • Apocaliptic Destruction. The land within 6 miles of the lair takes twice as long as normal to traverse, since destruction and debris cover the land.
  • Wither Vortex. A vortex of necrotic mist surrounds the area within 1 mile of the lair, rendering the area Lightly Obscured.
  • Withered Husks. Corpses within 6 miles of the lair become black with necrotic decay; their eyes and mouth glow with an unnatural violet hue.

If the wither dies, the damaged land remains, but other effects fade over 1d10 days.

Traits

Actions

Multiattack. The wither makes three Tentacle attacks and uses Swallow or Tractor Gaze.


Tentacle. Melee Attack Roll: +19, reach 30 ft. Hit: 28 (4d8 + 10) Bludgeoning damage. If the target is a Huge or smaller creature, the target has the Grappled condition (escape DC 20) from one of eight tentacles, and it has the Restrained condition until the grapple ends.


Swallow. Dexterity Saving Throw: DC 25, one creature Grappled by the wither (it can have up to six creatures swallowed at a time). Failure: 28 (4d8 + 10) Piercing damage. If the target is Large or smaller, it is swallowed and no longer Grappled. A swallowed creature has the Restrained condition, has Total Cover against attacks and other effects outside the wither, and takes 56 (16d6) Acid damage at the start of each of its turns.


 If the wither takes 60 damage or more on a single turn from a creature inside it, the wither must succeed on a DC 20 Constitution saving throw at the end of that turn or regurgitate all swallowed creatures, each of which falls in a space within 10 feet of the wither with the Prone condition. If the wither dies, any swallowed creature no longer has the Restrained condition and can escape from the corpse using 20 feet of movement, exiting Prone.


Tractor Gaze. Strength Saving Throw: DC 27, one creature the wither can see within 90 feet (the target succeeds automatically if it is Gargantuan). Failure: The target is pulled up to 30 feet straight towards the wither.


Legendary Actions

Legendary Action Uses: 3. Immediately after another creature's turn, The wither can expend a use to take one of the following actions. The wither regains all expended uses at the start of each of its turns.


Lash. The wither makes one Tentacle attack.


Tractor Gaze. The wither uses its Tractor Gaze. The wither can't take this action again until the start of its next turn.


Wither Cloud. Constitution Saving Throw: DC 27, each creature in a 60-foot Emmanation originating from the wither. Failure: 45 (10d8) Necrotic damage, and the target has the Blinded condition until the end of its next turn. Failure or Success: The wither can't take this action again until the start of its next turn.


Wither Storm's Lair

The wither storm's body is its own lair. The fleshy mix of organic and inorganic material deters or absorbs creatures that find their way inside.


Regional Effects

The region containing an wither storm's lair is warped by its presence, creating the following effects:


Apocaliptic Destruction. Death and destruction ravage the land within 6 miles of the lair, covering it with debris. Travel for creatures other than the wither takes twice the usual time in that area.

  • Wither Rings. A vortex of necrotic mist surrounds the area within 1 mile of the lair, rendering the area Lightly Obscured. Creatures that spend more than 1 hour within the mist have Disadvantage on Death Saving Throws.

    • If the wither dies or moves its lair elsewhere, these effects end immediately.
