Actions

Multiattack. The dragon can use its frightful presence. It then makes three attacks: one with its bite and two with its claws.


Bite. Melee Weapon Attack: +12 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 18 (2d10 + 7) piercing damage plus 7 (2d6) necrotic damage.


Claw. Melee Weapon Attack: +12 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 14 (2d6 + 7) slashing damage.


Tail. Melee Weapon Attack: +12 to hit, reach 15 ft., one target. Hit: 16 (2d8 + 7) bludgeoning damage.


Frightful Presence. Each creature of the dragon's choice that is within 120 feet of the dragon and aware of it must succeed on a DC 16 Wisdom saving throw or become frightened for 1 minute. A creature can repeat a saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success. If a creature's saving throw is successful or the effect ends for it, the creature is immune to the dragon's Frightful Presence for the next 24 hours.


Harmful Breath (Recharge 5-6). The dragon exhales putrid gas in a 60-foot cone. Each creature in the cone must make a DC 19 Constitution saving throw, taking 52 (15d6) necrotic damage on a failed save, or half as much on a successful one. The dragon then chooses a point it can see where the cone makes contact with the ground. That point becomes the center of a 10-foot-high, 10-foot-radius cylinder of lingering gas that disappears at the start of the dragon's next turn. Any creature that starts its turn in the cylinder takes 10 (3d6) necrotic damage.


Legendary Actions

The dragon can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action option can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. The dragon regains spent legendary actions at the start of its turn.


Tail attack. The dragon makes a tail attack.

Wing Attack (Costs 2 Actions). The dragon beats its wings. Each creature within 10 feet of the dragon must succeed on a DC 19 Dexterity saving throw or take 14 (2d6 + 7) bludgeoning damage and be knocked prone. The dragon can then fly up to half its flying speed.

Voidfire Ball (Costs 3 Actions). The dragon unleashes a ball of void-tainted necrotic energy that detonates in a 20-foot-radius sphere at a point it can see within 90 feet of it. Each creature within that sphere must make a DC 19 Constitution saving throw, taking 24 (7d6) necrotic damage on a failed save, or half as much on a successful one.


An Ender Dragon's Lair

Ender dragons originate from the Astral Plane, often lairing within wildspace systems or the petrified remains of dead gods. Their domains manifest as fragmented floating islands touched by decay, where plants wither away and aberrations roam freely. Between the fruit of chorus plants and the abundance of gateways, creatures within the lair don't stay in one place for long, be it willingly or by force.

 When an ender dragon enters the Material Plane, it does so to acquire knowledge or to conquer and claim territory. In such cases, they lair in grasslands and open fields where it can tear reality apart, bringing a part of its native domain to the world and ensure their presence is known. Ender dragons frequently clash with other powerful dragons. Gold dragons oppose their destructive influence, while red dragons compete with them for dominance, their conflicts often ending in devastation and death.

 At the center of an ender dragon's domain lies the dragon's resting place, surrounded by ten massive obsidian pillars arranged in a wide circle approximately 150 feet from the center. These pillars are of varying height, each between 60 to 150 feet tall and 20 to 40 feet wide in diameter at their base. Atop each pillar hovers a Large crystal object engraved with strange, glowing runes pulsing with alien magic.


Lair Actions

On initiative count 20 (losing initiative ties), the dragon takes a lair action to cause one of the following effects; the dragon can't use the same effect two rounds in a row:

  • Astral Fog. Void-chilled fog fills a 20-foot-radius sphere centered on a point the dragon chooses within 120 feet of it. The fog spreads around corners, and its area is lightly obscured. Each creature in the fog when it appears must make a DC 15 Constitution saving throw, taking 10 (3d6) cold damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one. A creature that ends its turn in the fog takes 10 (3d6) cold damage. The fog lasts until the dragon uses this lair actions again or until the dragon dies.
  • Crystal Healing. A crystal atop an obsidian pillar within 120 feet of the dragon begins channeling healing energy, creating a visible beam linking it to the dragon. At initiative count 20 on the next round, the dragon regains 60 hit points, and each creature within 20 feet of the pillar must make a successful DC 15 Constitution saving throw or take 10 (3d6) force damage. If the crystal is destroyed before the effect resolves, the dragon instead takes 15 (4d6) force damage, and the energy dissipates harmlessly. Each crystal is a Large object hovering above the obsidian pillar and has AC 11; 30 hit points; vulnerability to bludgeoning and thunder damage; and immunity to poison and psychic damage. When a crystal is destroyed, it explodes in a 20-foot-radius sphere. Any creature in that area must make a DC 15 Dexterity saving throw, taking 21 (6d6) force damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one.
  • End Displacement. A gateway lights up somewhere in the dragon's lair. One creature the dragon can see within 120 feet of it must succeed on a DC 15 Charisma saving throw or be teleported, appearing within 5 feet of the lit up gateway.

Regional Effect

The region containing a legendary ender dragon's lair is warped by the dragon's magic, which creates one or more of the following effects:

  • Aberrant Barrens. The land within 1 miles of the lair becomes barren and desolate, covering the land in pale yellow. Purple, coral-like chorus plants grow in abundance in the area. A creature that eats its fruit must make a DC 15 Constitution saving throw or be teleported up to 30 feet to a random unoccupied space.
  • End Gateways. Intradimensional gateways appear within 1 mile of the lair as floating, 15-foot-tall octahedrons reflecting an astral sky. Each gateway is linked to another. A creature or object that passes through one gateway emerges from a linked gateway within 5 feet of it. A gateway can be used by any creature and remains active indefinitely.
  • Whispers of the End. Endermen are unnaturally drawn to the lair, scouring the land within 1 miles of it.

If the dragon dies, the population of endermen diminishes and other effects fade over the course of 1d10 days.

Ender Dragon's Lair

Ender dragons dwell in the Astral Plane or invade open regions of other worlds, where their presence tears open reality bringing with it a piece of its alien domain and provokes violent conflicts with other dragons.


Regional Effects

The region containing an adult or ancient ender dragon's lair is warped by its presence, creating the following effects:


Aberrant Barrens. The land within 1 mile of the lair becomes barren and desolate, covering the land in pale yellow. Purple, coral-like chorus plants grow in abundance in the area. A creature that eats its fruit must make a DC 15 Constitution saving throw or be teleported up to 30 feet to a random unoccupied space, even through solid objects.

End Gateways. Intradimensional gateways appear within 1 mile of the lair as floating, 15-foot-tall octahedrons reflecting an astral sky. Each gateway is linked to another. A creature or object that passes through one gateway emerges from a linked gateway within 5 feet of it. A gateway can be used by any creature and remains active indefinitely.


If the dragon dies or moves its lair elsewhere, these effects end immediately.
