Creaking Link. The creaking's heart does not reside within its body, residing instead inside a tree's trunk within 120 feet of it. The creaking cannot move beyond 120 feet away from its heart, and if it is forcibly moved beyond that distance or becomes restrained, it is teleported to an unoccupied space within 10 feet of the tree housing its heart.


Plant Camouflage. The creaking has advantage on Dexterity (Steath) checks it makes in any terrain with ample obscuring vegetation.


Resin Restoration. If the creaking drops to 0 hit points and its heart remains intact, it doesn't die, and instead becomes unconscious for 1d4 rounds. While unconscious, fragments of bark and globs of resin fly between the creaking and the tree housing its heart, restoring 5 hit points at the end of each of its turns, up to its hit point maximum. The tree becomes coated in resin during this process. A creature that can see the creaking while it's unconscious can follow the trail of bark and resin to locate the heart's tree with a successful DC 14 Wisdom (Perception) check. The heart is a Tiny object and has AC 5; 25 hit points; and resistant to all damage except fire and slashing. If the heart is destroyed, the creaking is destroyed alongside it, crumbling into bark and resin.


Sight Lock. If a creature has direct line of sight to the creaking, the creaking becomes petrified, with the exception that it's still acutely aware of its surroundings. At the start of each of its turns, each creature that can see the creaking must make a successful DC 15 Constitution saving throw or involuntarily blink. If all such creatures blink, the creaking is freed from its petrified state until the start of its next turn.


Actions

Multiattack. The creaking makes two claw attacks.


Claw. Melee Weapon Attack: +4 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 7 (2d4 + 2) slashing damage plus 4 (1d8) necrotic damage.
