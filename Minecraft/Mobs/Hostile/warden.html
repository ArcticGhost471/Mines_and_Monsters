Aura of Darkness. Any creature that starts its turn within 60 feet of the warden must make a successful DC 21 Constitution saving throw or be surrounded by magical darkness until the end of its next turn. A creature with darkvision can't see through this darkness, and nonmagical light can't illuminate it.


Echolocation. The warden can't use its blindsight while deafened.


Keen Hearing and Smell. The warden has advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on hearing or smell.


Legendary Resistance (4/day). If the warden fails a saving throw, it can choose to succeed instead.


Magic Resistance. The warden has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects.


Relentless (Recharges after a Short or Long Rest). If the warden takes 35 damage or less that would reduce it to 0 hit points, it is reduced to 1 hit point instead.


Tunneler. The warden can burrow through solid rock at half its burrowing speed and leaves a 5-foot-wide, 10-foot-high tunnel in its wake.


Actions

Multiattack. The warden makes two slam attacks.


Slam. Melee Weapon Attack: +15 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 26 (4d8 + 8) bludgeoning damage plus 18 (4d8) necrotic damage.


Sonic Boom (Recharge 5-6). The warden releases an ethereal blast of thunderous energy from its chest at a creature it can sense within 60 feet. The creature takes 45 thunder damage, is pushed up to 10 feet away from the warden if it is Huge or smaller, and is knocked prone. The blast travels through solid objects to hit the creature.


Legendary Actions

The warden can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action option can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. The warden regains spent legendary actions at the start of its turn.


Move. The warden moves up to its speed.

Slam. The warden makes one slam attack.

Mind Alteration (Costs 2 Actions). The warden alters the mind of a creature it can sense within 120 feet. The creature must make succeed on a DC 17 Wisdom saving throw or take 13 (3d8) psychic damage and become confused for 1 minute. While confused in this way, the creature can't take bonus actions or reactions and must roll a d10 at the start of its turn to determine its behavior (as per the confusion spell). At the end of each of its turns, an affected target repeats the save, ending the effect on itself on a success.


A Warden's Lair

The warden's lies dormant within the ruins of an ancient underdark city. These ruins have been consumed by the sculk which arrived here by unknown means. With the exception of sculk creatures, the warden's lair is devoid of life as the sculk consumes those who die within the lair and many steer clear to avoid succumbing to the same fate.


Lair Actions

On initiative count 20 (losing initiative ties), the warden takes a lair action to cause one of the following effects; the dragon can't use the same effect two rounds in a row:

  • Aberrant Ping. The warden emits burst of psionic static. which travels up to 120 feet of it. Each creature within 120 feet of the warden must make a DC 15 Wisdom saving throw, taking 10 (3d6) psychic damage on a failed save or half as much on a successful one. The warden instantly becomes aware of the location of the closest creature that failed the save.
  • Sculk Jaw. Rows of teeth emerge from the sculk to bite at a creature the warden can sense within 120 feet of it.The warden makes one melee attack roll (+7 to hit) against each target. On a hit, the target takes 10 (3d6) piercing damage.

Regional Effect

The region containing a warden's lair is warped by its presence, creating the following effects:

  • Blinding Shrieks. Sculk Shriekers whithin 1 mile of the lair gain the warden's Aura of Darkness with a range of 120 feet whenever they shriek.
  • Sculk Corruption. Sculk infests the region within 1 mile of the lair and serve as the warden's ears.
  • Warden Call. The warden lies dormant within its lair until disturbed, called upon by Sculk Shriekers. After four shrieks less than an hour apart from each other, the warden crawls forth from the sculk, as if it was teleported, within 40 feet of the last shriek unless the warden is already within 90 feet of that shrieker.
  • Zone of Antilife. The area within 1 mile is devoid of life, either consumed by the sculk or avoiding the lair.

If the warden dies, the sculk remains and becomes inert, but other effects fade over 1d10 days.
