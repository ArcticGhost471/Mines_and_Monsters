Amphibious. The dragon can breathe air and water.


Legendary Resistance (3/day). If the dragon fails a saving throw, it can choose to succeed instead.


Actions

Multiattack. The dragon can use its Frightful Presence. It then makes three attacks: one with its bite and two with its claws.


Bite. Melee Weapon Attack: +11 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 17 (2d10 + 6) piercing damage plus 5 (1d10) Cold or Fire damage (the dragon's choice).


Claw. Melee Weapon Attack: +11 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 13 (2d6 + 6) slashing damage.


Tail. Melee Weapon Attack: +11 to hit, reach 15 ft., one target. Hit: 15 (2d8 + 6) bludgeoning damage.


Frightful Presence. Each creature of the dragon's choice that is within 120 feet of the dragon and aware of it must succeed on a DC 17 Wisdom saving throw or become frightened for 1 minute. A creature can repeat a saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success. If a creature's saving throw is successful or the effect ends for it, the creature is immune to the dragon's Frightful Presence for the next 24 hours.


Breath Weapon (Recharge 5-6). The dragon uses one of the following breath weapons.


Water Breath. The dragon exhales water in a 60-foot cone, or a 90-foot line that is 5 feet wide. Each creature in the chosen area must make a DC 19 Dexterity saving throw, taking 55 (10d10) Fire or Cold damage (the dragon's choice) on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one.

Flood Breath. The dragon expels water in a 30-foot cone. Each creature in that area must succed on a DC 19 Strength saving throw. On a failed save, the creature is pushed 60 feet away from the dragon and is knocked prone.


Legendary Actions

The dragon can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action option can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. The dragon regains spent legendary actions at the start of its turn.


Detect. The dragon makes a Wisdom (Perception) check.

Tail attack. The dragon makes a tail attack.

Aura of Healing (Costs 2 Action). The dragon makes shimmering water swirl around itself or another creature it can see within 60 feet of it. The target magically regains 10 (3d6) Hit Points, and gains 10 (3d6) Temporary Hit Points. The dragon can't take this action again until the start of its next turn.


An Aquamarine Dragon's Lair

Aquamarine dragons reside underwater, hiding their lairs in or near the sea. They are content with flooded caverns or aquatic ruins, but some aquamarine dragons seek majestic coral reefs to care for and make their homes in. They may choose to craft their own coral palace by carefully relocating corals they deem to be in vulnerable areas.


Lair Actions

On initiative count 20 (losing initiative ties), the dragon takes a lair action to cause one of the following effects; the dragon can't use the same effect two rounds in a row:

  • Fog. The dragon creates fog as though it had cast the fog cloud spell. The fog lasts until initiative count 20 on the next round.
  • Ocean Currents. The dragon creates an underwater current in a 20-foot Cube within 120 feet of the dragon in a direction of the dragon's choice, including upward or downward. Each creature within 5 feet of the cube must make a DC 15 Strength saving throw or be dragged up to 20 feet in the direction of the current.
  • One With the Waves. The dragon blends in with the waves as though it had cast the invisibility spell, and moves up to half its speed.

Regional Effect

The region containing a legendary aquamarine dragon's lair is warped by the dragon's magic, which creates one or more of the following effects:

  • Clear Water. The water sources within 1 mile of the lair are supernaturally pure and clear.
  • Servants of the Seas. Fish, other aquatic Beasts, and snakes within 6 miles of the dragon's lair serve as the dragon's eyes and ears.
  • Sun and Storms. Once per day, the dragon can alter the weather in a 6-mile radius centered on its lair. The dragon doesn't need to be outdoors; otherwise the effect is identical to the control weather spell.

If the dragon dies, these effects fade over the course of 1d10 days.

Aquamarine Dragon Lairs

Aquamarine dragons make their homes near or under the sea. Some lair in underwater palaces made of corals while others dwell in flooded caverns.


Regional Effects

The region containing an adult or Adult aquamarine dragon's lair is warped by its presence, creating the following effects:


Aquatic Servants. Tiny aquatic Beasts magically gain the ability to understand Draconic and can communicate telepathically with the dragon while within 1 mile of the lair.

Sun and Storms. While in its lair, the dragon can cast Control Weather, requiring no Material components and using the same spellcasting ability as its Spellcasting action.


If the dragon dies or moves its lair elsewhere, these effects end immediately.
