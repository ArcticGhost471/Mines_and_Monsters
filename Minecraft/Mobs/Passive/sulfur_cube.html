Sulfur Cube

12 (+1) 13 (+1) 16 (+3) 1 (-5) 3 (-4) 6 (-2)

Damage Resistances fire

Damage Immunities acid, lightning, slashing

Condition Immunities charmed, deafened, exhaustion, frightened, prone

Senses darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 6

Languages —

Challenge 1/4 (50 XP)

Proficiency Bonus +2


Docile Trance. When the cube has engulfed an object, it enters a trance-like state. While in this trance, the cube's speed becomes 0, it can only be moved by other creatures, and can't use actions, bonus actions, or reactions. In addition, the cube becomes immune to all forms of damage except fire, force, poison, and psychic damage.


Ooze Cube. The cube takes up its entire space. Other creatures can enter the space, but a creature that does so is subjected to the cube's Engulf and has disadvantage on the saving throw.

 Creatures inside the cube can be seen but have total cover.

 A creature within 5 feet of the cube can take an action to pull a creature or object out of the cube. Doing so requires a successful DC 12 Strength check, and the creature making the attempt takes 2 (1d4) acid damage.

 The cube can hold only one creature of its size or smaller if it is Medium or smaller.


Property Absorption. When the cube has engulfed an object of its size or smaller, it gains one of the following properties based on the object within itself; when hit with a critical hit, it expels the engulfed object in an unoccupied space within 5 feet of it:

  • Bouncy. When the cube engulfs wood, it becomes bouncy. Whenever a creature hits it with an attack roll, it bounces up to 30 feet in a direction of the creature's choice.
  • Desecrated. When the cube engulfs graveyard dirt or other such material, it gains a cursed magical aura. It casts suggestion on creatures within range (save DC 13).
  • Entangle. When the cube engulfs vegetable matter or resin, any object inside it becomes harder to pull out. A creature making the attempt must succeed on a DC 15 Strength check or become grappled. A creature can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on a success.
  • Explosive. When the cube engulfs explosive material, it becomes unstable. Whenever the cube takes fire damage, it emits an audible hiss within 30 feet, exploding in a 20-foot-sphere of fire at the start of its next turn. The cube is destroyed. Each creature within the sphere must succeed on a DC 13 Dexterity saving throw, taking 10 (3d6) fire damage on a failed save, or half as much on a successful save.
  • Heavy. When the cube engulfs metal, it becomes extremely heavy. The cube's weight magically becomes 30 pounds and sinks when in water. When falling, its fall speed is now double. A creature that finds themselves under the cube when it falls must succeed on a DC 15 Dexterity saving throw or take 10 (3d6) bludgeoning damage. A creature that succeed on the save moves to an unoccupied space within 5 feet of the cube.
  • Hot. When the cube engulfs molten rock, it becomes extremely hot to the touch. A creature that touches or makes an attack roll within 5 feet of the cube takes 5 (2d4) fire damage.
  • Slippery. When the cube engulfs ice or snow, it becomes slippery. A creature that attempts to grapple the cube must succeed on a DC 15 Dexterity saving throw or push the cube 10 feet in the direction opposite to them.
  • Solid. When the cube engulfs earth, it becomes solid. A creature can move the cube 1 foot for every 2 feet of movement.
  • Sticky. When the cube engulfs viscous matter, it becomes sticky and it clings to walls, floors, and ceilings. While on a wall or ceiling, the cube drops after 1d4 rounds.
  • Toxic. When the cube engulfs fungal matter, its spores mix with the cube's sulfurous cube. The cube constantly releases noxious gas around itself. Each creature within 5 feet of the cube must make a DC 12 Constitution saving throw, taking 3 (1d6) poison damage on a failed save, or half as much on a success. On a failed save, the creature is also poisoned for 1 minute. The gas' range increases by 5 feet, up to ten 10-foot Cubes, and the poison damage increases by 1d6 every 1 minute. A strong wind disperses the gas for 1 minute.
  • Wall. When the cube engulfs stone or minerals, it becomes heavy and difficult to move. A creature can use an action to make a DC 10 Strength check, moving the cube up to 10 feet on a success. Creatures cannot enter the cube's space. The cube sinks when in water.
  • Weightless. When the cube engulfs wool or other lightweight matter, the cube's weight magically becomes as light as a feather. When falling, it falls as if it were under the effects of the feather fall spell. The cube doesn't trigger any traps that trigger when stepped on.

Actions

Pseudopod. Melee Weapon Attack: +3 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 4 (1d6 + 1) bludgeoning damage.


Engulf. The cube moves up to its speed. While doing so, it can enter Medium or smaller creatures' spaces. Whenever the cube enters a creatures' space, the creature must make a DC 12 Dexterity saving throw.

 On a successful save, the creature can choose to be pushed 5 feet back or to the side of the cube. A creature that chooses not to be pushed suffers the consequences of a failed saving throw.

 On a failed save, the cube enters the creature's space, and the creature takes 2 (1d4) acid damage and is engulfed. An engulfed creature can't breathe, is restrained, and takes 5 (2d4) acid damage at the start of each of the cube's turns. When the cube moves, the engulfed target moves with it.

 An engulfed target can try to escape by taking an action to make a DC 12 Strength check. On a successful check, the creature escapes and enters a space of its choice within 5 feet of the cube.


Reactions

Split. When a cube that is Medium is subjected to lightning or slashing damage, it splits into two new cubes if it has at least 10 hit points. Each new cube has hit points equal to half the original cube's, rounded down. New cube are one size smaller than the original cube.
