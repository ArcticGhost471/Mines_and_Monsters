Spider Climb. The spider can climb difficult surfaces, including upside down on ceiling, without needing to make an ability check.


Web Sense. While in contact with a web, the spider knows the exact location of any other creature in contact with the same web.


Web Walker. The spider ignores movement restrictions caused by webbing.


Actions

Bite. Melee Weapon Attack: +3 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 4 (1d6 + 1) piercing damage.


Web (Recharge 5-6). Ranged Weapon Attack: +4 to hit, range 30/60 ft., one creature. Hit: The target is restrained by webbing. As an action, the restrained target can make a DC 12 Strength check, bursting the webbing on a success. The webbing can also be attacked and destroyed (AC 10; hp 5; vulnerability to fire damage; immunity to bludgeoning, poison, and psychic damage).


Variant: Enchanted Duskhunter Spiders

Spiders that linger in areas of concentrated magic can become enchanted and gain innate magical abilities. This phenomenon is a common occurrence for spiders that live near places where magically adept creatures reside, leading the spider to absorb the residual magical energy in the atmosphere.

 Choose or roll an enchantment from the Spider Enchantments table to determine the enchantment on a group of enchanted spiders. These enchantments function as an enchanted spider's trait.


Spider Enchantments

d4 Enchantment
1 Swiftness. The spider's movement speed is extended by 10 ft.
2 Strength. When a spider hits with any melee weapon, the weapon deals an extra 1d6 piercing damage.
3 Regeneration. The spider regains 3 hit points at the start of its turn if it has at least one hit point.
4 Invisibility. The spider is invisible.
