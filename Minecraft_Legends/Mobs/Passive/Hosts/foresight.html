Innate Spellcasting. Foresight's spellcasting ability is Wisdom (spell save DC 25). She can innately cast the following spells, requiring no material components:


At will: detect evil and good, sending, pass without trace

3/day each: arcane eye, locate creature

1/day each: foresight (self only), gate, scrying


Legendary Resistance (3/day). If Foresight fails a saving throw, it can choose to succeed instead.


Magic Resistance. Foresight has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects.


Actions

Multiattack. Foresight makes three psychic rend attacks.


Psychic Rend. Ranged Spell Attack: +16 to hit, range 120 ft., one target. Hit: 32 (4d10 + 10) psychic damage.


Etherealness. Foresight enters the Ethereal Plane from the Material Plane, or vice versa. She is visible on the Material Plane while she is in the Border Ethereal, and vice versa, yet she can't affect or be affected by anything on the other plane.


Legendary Actions

Foresight can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action option can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. Foresight regains spent legendary actions at the start of its turn.


Ethereal Form. Foresight uses Etherealness.

Psychic Rend. Foresight makes one Psychic Rend attack.

Flames of Creation (Costs 2 Actions). A column of celestial fire descends upon a point in the ground Foresight can see within 60 feet that becomes the center of a 60-foot-high, 20-foot-radius cylinder of fire. Each creature in that cylinder must succeed on a DC 25 Dexterity saving throw or take 18 (4d8) radiant damage.


The Host's Lair

The Hosts take residence in the Well of Fate, a celestial settlement located in the Ethereal Plane. It exists in the Border Ethereal where the hosts can peer into the Material Plane without interfering in the affairs of mortals.


Regional Effect

The region containing the Host's lair is warped by its magic, creating one or more of the following effects:

  • Anomalous Existance. The lair has a physical presence on both the Material Plane and the Border Ethereal at once, appearing as an elaborate temple in the Material Plane. A creature that uses the etherealness spell while at the temple is transported into the lair in the Border Ethereal.
  • Ethereal Presence. Creatures not native to the Ethereal Plane have a translucent appearance while in the lair.
  • Portals of Fate. Once per day, the Host can open a portal linking to another plane of existence. They don't need to know the name of a specific creature, otherwise the effect is identical to the gate spell.

If the Host dies, these effects fade over the course of 1d10 days.

The Host's Lair

Regional Effects

The region containing the Host's lair is warped by its magic, creating the following effects:


  • Anomalous Existance. The lair has a physical presence on both the Material Plane and the Border Ethereal at once, appearing as an elaborate temple in the Material Plane. A creature that uses the Etherealness spell while at the temple is transported into the lair in the Border Ethereal.
  • Portals of Fate. While in its lair, the Host can cast Gate, requiring no Material components and using the same spellcasting ability as its Spellcasting action. When casting the spell this way, the Host can open a portal next to a creature of their choosing without needing to know the creature's name.
