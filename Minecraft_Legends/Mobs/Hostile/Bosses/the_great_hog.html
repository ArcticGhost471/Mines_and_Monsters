Innate Spellcasting. The Great Hog's spellcasting ability is Charisma (spell save DC 22). He can innately cast the following spells, requiring no material components:


At will: detect magic, thunderous smite, lightning arrow

3/day: lightning bolt, staggering smite, wall of fire

1/day: control weather, gate


Legendary Resistance (3/day). If The Great Hog fails a saving throw, he can choose to succeed instead.


Magic Resistance. The Great Hog has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects.


Magic Weapons. The Great Hog's weapon attacks are magical.


Zombification Plague. If The Great Hog remains outside the Lower Planes for 30 minutes without exposure to Nether Wart, his body quickly deteriorates into undead flesh and becomes a zombified piglin. A zombified piglin can die permanently outside of its native plane.


Actions

Multiattack. The Great Hog makes three spear attack or two Quarterstaff attacks.


Quarterstaff. Melee Weapon Attack: +17 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 20 (3d6 + 10) bludgeoning damage plus 13 (2d12) lightning damage, or 23 (3d8 + 10) bludgeoning damage plus 13 (2d12) lightning damage if wielded with two hands.


Spear. Melee or Ranged Weapon Attack: +17 to hit, reach 10 ft. or range 60/240 ft., one target. Hit: 20 (3d6 + 10) piercing damage, or 23 (3d8 + 10) piercing damage if used with two hands to make a melee attack.


Legendary Actions

The Great Hog can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action option can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. The Great Hog regains spent legendary actions at the start of their turn.


Move. The Great Hog moves up to its speed.

Spear. The Great Hog makes one spear attack.

Lightning Aura (Cost 2 Actions). The Great Hog strikes the ground with his staff, electrically charging the ground. All other creatures on the ground within 60 feet of the Great Hog must succeed on a DC 25 Strength saving throw or take 13 (3d8) lightning damage and become stunned until the end of their next turn.



The Great Hog's Lair

The Great Hog's lair is a mighty fortress, the Obsidian Fortress, which is on the second layer of Gehenna, Chamada. The Obsidian Fortress is unforgiving to those the Great Hog deems unworthy and its defenses are relentless in the destruction any resistance to the Great Hog's rule. It is believed that the fortress rose from the volcanoes of Gehenna, spat out alongside the Great Hog for destruction and conquest.


Lair Actions

On initiative count 20 (losing initiative ties), The Great Hog takes a lair action to cause one of the following effects; The Great Hog can't use the same effect two rounds in a row:

  • Nether Portal. The Great Hog creates an imploding portal centered on a point he can see within 120 feet. Any creature within 20 feet of the portal must succeed on a DC 15 Strength saving throw or be pulled up to 20 feet into the portal. Creatures pulled into the portal are transported to a fiery demiplane until the start of the Great Hog's next turn, at which point they take 21 (6d6) fire damage and reappear in an unoccupied spaces of the Great Hog's choice within 120 feet of the Great Hog.
  • Incite the Horde. Until the next initiative count 20, all piglins and hoglins within the lair are enraged, causing them to have advantage on melee weapon attack rolls and causing attack rolls to have advantage against them.

Regional Effect

The region containing The Great Hog's lair is warped by his magic, creating one or more of the following effects:

  • Raging Furnace. Volcanoes within 6 miles of the lair rage twice as fiercely.
  • Nether Corruption. The land within 6 miles of the lair is corrupted by his presence, becoming barren and reminiscent of the Abyss.
  • Thunderstorms. Thunderstorms rage within 6 miles of the lair.

If The Great Hog dies, these effects fade over the course of 1d10 days.

Legendary Actions

Lightning Aura. Strength Saving Throw: DC 25, each creature in a 30-foot Emanation originating from the Great Hog. Failure: 27 (6d8) Lightning damage, and the target has the Stunned condition until the end of their next turn. Success: Half damage only. Failure or Success: The Great Hog can't take this action again until the start of its next turn.


Smash. The Great Hog makes one Greatstaff attack.


Swing. The Great Hog makes one Netherite Spear attack.


The Great Hog's Lair

The Great Hog's lair is a mighty fortress, the Obsidian Fortress, found on the second layer of Gehenna, believed to have been spat out by the layer itself.


Regional Effects

The region containing The Great Hog's lair is warped by his magic, creating the following effects:


Nether Corruption. The land within 1 mile of the lair is corrupted by his presence, becoming barren and reminiscent of the Abyss.

Nether Storm. Volcanoes and Thunderstorms rage within 1 mile of the lair. The area is Lightly Obscured.


If The Great Hog dies or moves his lair elsewhere, these effects end immediately.
