Magic Resistance. The Devourer has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects.


Magic Weapons. The Devourer's weapon attacks are magical.


Rot. Any creature other than a piglin that starts its turn within 10 feet of the Devourer must succeed on a DC 22 Constitution saving throw or be poisoned until the start of the creature's next turn.


Zombification Plague. If The Devourer remains outside the Lower Planes for 30 minutes without exposure to Nether Wart, his body quickly deteriorates into undead flesh and becomes a zombified piglin. A zombified piglin can die permanently outside of its native plane.


Actions

Multiattack. The Devourer uses Acid Belch once and makes two slam attacks.


Slam. Melee Weapon Attack: +14 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 21 (3d8 + 8) bludgeoning damage.


Acid Belch. The Devourer targets one creature it can see within 120 feet of it. The target must succeed on a DC 22 Dexterity saving throw or take 28 (8d6) acid damage.


Rumbling Groan (1/day). The Devourer lets out a trembling groan that resounds around the battlefield. All creatures within 30 feet of him that can hear him must make a DC 22 Constitution saving throw. On a failure, a creature drops to 0 hit points. On a success, a creature takes 59 (17d6) thunder damage.


Legendary Actions

The Devourer can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action option can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. The Devourer regains spent legendary actions at the start of their turn.


Attack. The Devourer makes one slam attack.

Targetted Belch. The Devourer uses Acid Belch.

Vomit (Cost 2 Actions). The Devourer vomits in a 30-foot cone. Each creature in that cone must succeed DC 22 Dexterity saving throw or take 18 (4d8) acid damage.



The Devourer's Lair

The Devourer's is a mushroom forest found to the first layer of Gehenna, Khalas, known as the Warped Forest. The Warped Forest stands out in the volcanic layer of Khalas, its greens and blues a contrast to the oranges and greys of the layer. Though it's vague similarity to the material plane makes it seem like a safe haven from the dangers of gehenna, the Warped Forest noxious atmosphere reflects the gluttony of The Devourer. Through its toxic hazard, The Devourer's horde; consisting of piglins, sporebacks, and lava lauchers; stand tall alongside him with unwavering respect.


Lair Actions

On initiative count 20 (losing initiative ties), The Devourer takes a lair action to cause one of the following effects; The Devourer can't use the same effect two rounds in a row:

  • Elevated Plateau. The Devourer chooses an area within the lair that is no larger in any dimension than 100 feet. Until the next initiative count 20, the area is raised up to 40 feet by thick vines. The Devourer can then choose to propel himself up to the new elevation and attack targets that find themselves there.
  • Gust of Spores. A gust of netherspores is propelled in 20-foot cube within 120 feet of the Devourer in a random direction. Each creature in the cube must make a DC 15 Strength saving throw or be pushed up to 30 feet in the direction of the gust.
  • Mushroom Grenades. The Devourer grabs a handful of explosive mushrooms and hurls them, targetting up to three creatures he can see within 120 feet of it. The Devourer makes one ranged attack roll (+8 to hit) against each target. On a hit, the target takes 10 (3d6) poison damage.

Regional Effect

The region containing The Devourer's lair is warped by his magic, creating one or more of the following effects:

  • Heated Pools. Warm pools of stew-like water are spread sparcely within 1 mile of the lair, releasing clouds of steam that renders the surrounding area lightly obscured.
  • Twisting Vines. Vines grow into thickets forming passages within 1 mile of the lair. The thickets act as 10-foot-high, 5-foot-thick walls that block line of sight. Creatures can move through the thickets, with every 1 foot a creature moves costing it 4 feet of movement. A creature in the thickets must make a DC 15 Dexterity saving throw once each round it's in contact with the thickets or take 3 (1d6) piercing damage from thorns.

    •  Each 10-foot wall of thickets has AC 5, 30 hit points, resistance to bludgeoning and piercing damage, vulnerability to fire damage, and immunity to psychic and thunder damage.

  • Warped Forests. Green tree-like mushrooms grow within 1 mile of the lair, causing the lair to stand out against its environment and poisoning the air around them. Whenever a creature other than The Devourer and its allies finishes a Long Rest in that area, it must succeed on a DC 15 Constitution saving throw or be poisoned for 1 hour.

If The Devourer dies, these effects fade over the course of 1d10 days.
